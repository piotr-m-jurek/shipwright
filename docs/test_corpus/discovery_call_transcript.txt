Discovery Call — Leave Management System
Date: 2024-11-08
Attendees: Sarah Chen (Product), Marcus Webb (CTO), Joanna Kowalski (HR Director),
           Tom Adeyemi (Engineering Lead), Priya Sharma (HR Manager)

---

[Recording starts mid-conversation]

MARCUS: ...right, so the spreadsheet thing has been a disaster. We had three people
show up to work on the same day last month because nobody could see the approved
leave properly. It's embarrassing at our size.

SARAH: That's exactly the kind of thing we're trying to fix. So let's go through
the main flows. Joanna, from HR's perspective, what's the single most painful part
of the current process?

JOANNA: Honestly? The delegation problem. When a manager goes on leave, their
reports have no one to approve their requests. We had a situation in August where
someone needed emergency leave approved and their manager was on holiday in Greece,
completely unreachable. The person had to escalate to me personally. That needs
to be solved properly — when a manager is going to be away, they should be able
to designate someone to act in their place. And that delegate should get all the
pending approvals automatically reassigned. It's a core workflow, not a nice-to-have.

TOM: Agreed. That's something we'll definitely need to build. We should make sure
the acceptance criteria for that are really detailed before development starts —
who can be a delegate, does the delegate need to be from the same team, does the
delegation apply to requests submitted before or after the delegation was set up,
what happens when the delegate is also on leave...

SARAH: Good point, we haven't written those up yet. Let me add that to the open
questions.

PRIYA: Can I raise something? A lot of our employees are not at their desks all day.
The warehouse staff in Berlin especially — they're on the floor, they check their
phones, not laptops. The current spreadsheet actually kind of works for them because
they can open it on mobile. If we build a web-only tool, we're going to lose that.

MARCUS: Yeah, I was going to bring this up. I actually spoke to the CEO about this
last week and she was very clear — she wants a mobile app. She said, and I'm quoting
here, "I'm not approving a system our warehouse staff can't use from their phones."
That's a hard requirement from the top.

TOM: That's going to affect the timeline significantly. A proper mobile app is a
different scope.

MARCUS: I know, but it's not optional. We might need to revisit the timeline. Sarah,
the PRD currently says mobile is out of scope for V1 — that needs to change.

SARAH: [pause] Okay. I'll update that. Mobile is in scope.

TOM: We should decide — native app or responsive web? Because responsive web
is a lot faster to ship.

MARCUS: Responsive web is fine to start. It doesn't have to be a native app.
Just has to work on a phone.

PRIYA: While we're talking about things people need to know — notifications. We
need the system to send notifications. People miss emails all the time. Some of
our managers prefer Slack. Can we do both?

TOM: That's something we'd need to specify. Are we talking email, Slack, in-app,
all three? Because each one is different engineering work.

SARAH: Let's put that down as something to decide. The PRD just says "notifications"
right now without specifying the channel. We need to pin that down.

JOANNA: From HR's perspective — email at minimum. If Slack is easy to add, great,
but email is non-negotiable.

TOM: Email is straightforward. Slack would need a Slack app, that's more work.
In-app notifications are easy if we're already building the web app.

MARCUS: Let's say email plus in-app for V1, and we can revisit Slack later.

SARAH: Agreed. I'll update the PRD.

[Discussion continues about BambooHR API details, redacted for brevity]

TOM: One last thing — where are we hosting this? We've been using AWS us-east-1
for most of our internal tools but I know there were some questions about data
residency given we're dealing with employee health data in sick leave records.

JOANNA: That's actually covered in the RFP. Everything has to stay in the EU.
AWS eu-central-1 or eu-west-1. Tom, make sure engineering knows that before
anyone starts spinning up infrastructure.

TOM: Got it. I'll flag that with the team.

MARCUS: Alright, I think we have enough to move forward. Sarah, can you update
the PRD with what we agreed today?

SARAH: Will do. Main updates: mobile in scope, delegation workflow needs detailed
acceptance criteria, notifications are email plus in-app for V1.
